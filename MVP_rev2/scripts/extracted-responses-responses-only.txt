Context: financial-sidekick
================================================================================
Inflation can erode the purchasing power of your savings, meaning that the money you save today may buy less in the future. For example, if inflation is at 3% per year, a dollar saved today will only have the equivalent purchasing power of about 97 cents next year. 

To combat this, it’s important to allocate your savings in a way that seeks to outpace inflation. This typically involves investing in assets like stocks or real estate, which have the potential for higher returns over time compared to keeping cash in a low-interest savings account. Ensuring a mix of savings and investments can help preserve your purchasing power in the long run.

This process ensures that savings are prioritized while respecting the limits on how much can be shifted from wants.
